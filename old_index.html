Honda Introduced the third generation Integra in 1993 at the Honda Verno locations in Japan and was sold internationally in 1994. Its front end design was unique and was dubbed "spider eyes" by enthusiasts. The Integra was imported to the United States and was sold as the Acura Integra. The spider eye front end was proven unpopular in Japan, so all Japanese Integras were given a facelift that featured more conventional flat headlights. The Integra has five trims: RS, LS, GS, GS-R, and Type R.
